SAE Aero Design

Texas A&M University


As a Structures and Material Science Designer for the 2022-23 SAE Aero Regular class season, there were many design challenges that I got to work on. The plane design for the 2022 season is particularly impressive because the 18-foot wingspan and large fuselage capacity allowed the plane to carry weight capable of reaching the FAA limit of 55 pounds and made this plane the biggest in Texas A&M history. In addition, our team had to make sure all design decisions followed the annually updated ruleset.

| Analysis & Verification

  • Flight modelling – OpenRocket & RASAero II predict apogee 12,172 ft AGL @ Mach 0.924 with 5.53 cal stability margin.
  • FEA – ANSYS Static Structural on orthotropic lay‑ups shows ≥ 2.5 × limit‑load safety factor.

| Integration & Testing Milestones

  • ASTM-D695-91 – Compression Testing not possible due to our body tube’s 4” diameter barely exceeding testing apparatus capability. In addition, there is not enough material to test for buckling failure criteria with the recommended 10:1 length-to-diameter ratio.
  • ASTM-D3039-95 - Two flat samples were tested with mutually perpendicular orientations to obtain axial & circumferntial strength values
  • ASTM-D2290 - Out-of-plane tensile hoop strength properties for body tubes
  • Static fire & Flight Readiness Review – June 2025.


| Impact & Lessons Learned

This was an excellent experience in regards to managing a team, composites, and setting the foundation for an efficient workflow that enabled our structures team to accelerate progress in the future. Most importantly, running design reviews as “teaching sprints” boosted team‑wide analytical intuition — raising quality faster than any solo effort.



TL;DR – Led structures on a 10k ft hybrid rocket, validated 2.5 × SF composites, mentored 12 engineers, and will put the vehicle on the pad at IREC 2025.
